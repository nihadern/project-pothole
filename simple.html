Today, no one has a full picture of which roads are bad. Repairs happen when people complain — not where they're needed most. Project Pothole changes that.

How it works, in three steps

No new machines. No expensive surveys. Just photos, smart software, and one simple map.

1

Photograph the roads

A phone on the dashboard, or street-view photos already online, capture the road as vehicles drive normally.

2

AI spots the damage

Software looks at each photo and automatically marks every pothole, crack and broken edge — in seconds.

3

Every road gets a score

The result is one live map showing which roads are good, which need attention, and which are bad — ranked worst-first.

Why it matters

Safer roads

Bad road surfaces cause crashes. Finding and fixing them early means fewer accidents and lives saved.

Saves money

Fixing a small crack early costs a fraction of rebuilding a collapsed road later. The same budget covers far more.

Fair & data-driven

Repairs go where roads are actually worst — decided by data, not by who complains the loudest.

Transparent

Before-and-after photos for every repair. Show citizens and the Assembly exactly what was fixed, where, and when.

Let's start with one district.

A 500 km pilot on the State Highways in Malappuram and Thrissur — at no upfront cost to the department. See the whole network on one map within weeks.
