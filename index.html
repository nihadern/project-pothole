The Platform

A command view of Kerala's roads — built for the people who run them

Condition data for 29,500 km lives today in field notebooks, site visits, and institutional memory. Project Pothole brings it into one continuously updated picture — full visibility, evidence-based prioritisation, and a clear record of work delivered.

Total network visibility
The live condition of every State Highway and District Road on one map — no waiting years between manual surveys to learn where attention is needed.
Evidence-based prioritisation
Each repair ranked by an objective condition score and dated imagery, so budget flows to the segments that need it most — and the decision is defensible.
A record of work delivered
Before-and-after imagery for every repair. Show citizens, the Assembly, and funding bodies exactly what was fixed, where, and when.
More road per rupee
Catching deterioration early means preventive resurfacing instead of full reconstruction — the same maintenance budget covers far more kilometres.

Processing Pipeline

Image to condition score, end to end

Street imagery and app-fleet sensor data both run the same pipeline into one ground truth per segment.

01
Mapillary Pull
Download existing Kerala street imagery from Meta's open platform — geotagged frames across major corridors.
02
AI Detection
YOLO-v8 trained on Indian roads classifies potholes, cracking, edge failure and rutting per frame.
03
Segment Score
Defects grouped per 100 m of road by GPS; several photos combine into one agreed condition score.
04
Twin Update
Scores written to PostGIS, the map updates, the priority queue re-ranks, gaps are flagged.

Why Mapillary: openly licensed (CC BY-SA), fully downloadable via API, and it grows as our own fleet adds coverage — a living dataset, not a one-time snapshot.

01
App Fleet
One app on KSRTC buses, auto-rickshaws, contracted surveyors and PWD vehicles — anyone we equip. Offline-first, passive recording.
02
Accelerometer IRI
3-axis motion becomes a roughness-index proxy, ±0.8 m/km vs. a laser profilometer.
03
Consensus
Minimum 3 passes before a score updates; outliers filtered; camera frames stored as evidence.
04
Twin Update
Verified score fused within minutes; the KSRTC fleet alone gives systematic SH coverage every 30 days.

Not crowdsourcing: the same app rides KSRTC buses, autos, surveyor vehicles and PWD fleet. Equip 50 buses and the entire State Highway network is re-scanned every month — automatically.
